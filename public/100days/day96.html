Bash scripting (part 3)

Processes and jobs

set -o monitor to see the “Done” message after a background process is done

grep -rw “ajbqf” *

^Z stop

bg or fg to resume the process in background or foreground, in foreground the terminal window will be tied up

grep -rw “ajbqf” *

^Z

grep -rw “126t3” *

^Z

bg %2 => will resume the second grep process in background

fg %1 => will resume the first grep process in the foreground

%1 - refers to the process that was stopped first or sent to bg first

Can also use kill %1

Other commands:

Wait - to do something after waiting for a process to finish

Jobs - to list all jobs/processes that are running

ps - to list processes that are running -a -u <user> -x -e
